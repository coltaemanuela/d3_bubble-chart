The bubble chart depicts financial data of the proposed projects of the agencies/departments of the US administration, between 2010 and 2017.
It presents the dynamics of the agencies project proposal and and is illustrating 3 dimensions: project year, project value, project number. It offers insight of over the evolution in their number over the years, and their value.


Each agency is represented by a different color.Bubbles are positioned depeding on their Average Project Costs expressed in $M and depending on the year of the proposed projects. The size of the bubble depends on the number of proposed projects per year. The position of the bubble depends on the value of the projects.


The upper positions on the Y axis and the bigger the bubble size, the more performant the department in attracting and absorbing allocated funds and in implementing their projects and achieving their goals.


If you are interested in one department activity,move the pointer to a bubble. All the same color bubbles will stay on the chart, while all the other colors bubbles will become transparent.
On the left, a div colored with the same bubble color will appear with more details about the bubble.
After moving the pointer, the div will disappear and all the other bubbles will become visible again.
